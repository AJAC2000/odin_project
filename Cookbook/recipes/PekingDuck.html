Peking Duck!

Description

Peking Duck, a culinary masterpiece from Beijing, China, is renowned for its crispy skin and succulent meat. The dish involves roasting a whole duck until the skin turns golden and crisp, then thinly slicing the meat and serving it with steamed pancakes, scallions, cucumber, and hoisin sauce, creating a delightful combination of textures and flavors.

Ingredients

  • Whole duck
  • Salt
  • Honey
  • Soy sauce
  • Rice vinegar
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Scallions (spring onions), sliced
  • Cucumber, cut into strips
  • Pancakes or tortillas

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Rinse the duck under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels.
  3. Sprinkle salt inside and outside the duck, rubbing it gently to ensure even distribution.
  4. In a small bowl, combine honey, soy sauce, and rice vinegar to create a glaze.
  5. Brush the glaze mixture all over the duck, making sure to coat it thoroughly.
  6. Place the duck on a rack in a roasting pan, breast side up.
  7. Roast the duck in the preheated oven for about 2 to 2 1/2 hours, or until the skin is crispy and golden brown.
  8. Remove the duck from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes.
  9. Carve the duck into thin slices, including both the crispy skin and tender meat.
  10. Serve the Peking Duck slices in pancakes or tortillas, along with sliced scallions and cucumber strips for added freshness and crunch.
  11. Roll up the pancakes or tortillas, and enjoy your homemade Peking Duck!
